Silver Needle

White tea

Bai Hao Yin Zhen is the most exquisite variety of white tea. It is picked in early spring in the high hills of Fujian province. Only the youngest, still unfurled upper leaves covered with white fur are picked for this tea, that is why it is called “White Furred Silver Needles.”

The harvested tea is carefully dried in natural conditions and is subjected to no further processing. This is why it preserves the natural flavor and fine aroma of fresh tea leaves. Bai Hao Yin Zhen is the world leader in concentration of healthy elements: it is rich with antioxidants, vitamins of groups B, С and РР, microelements and amino acids. It contains less caffeine than other varieties. White tea strengthens the immune system, improves blood-clotting ability and contributes to the healing of wounds. It also prevents development of heart and vascular diseases, cancers and caries.

The almond yellow brew has a light aroma with a fine nutmeg note, like honey plant’s. The flavor is sweet and fresh, but not harsh, with a nice slightly sour taste.

  • Where to buy: SILVER NEEDLE WHITE TEA
  • Category: White tea

Longjing

Green Tea

In China Longjing is called the tea of four perfections: flavor, color, aroma and form. Due to the specific method of processing the most popular green tea in the world, the tea’s natural shade and freshness is preserved.

Longjing is rich in vitamins and minerals and has high concentration of EGCG — the strongest natural antioxidant. If used regularly, green tea slows down the aging process, lowers the risk of cancer diseases and stabilizes blood pressure.

Longjing is good for any time of the day — it invigorates, clarifies the consciousness and helps overcome fatigue and dizziness.

The light green brew has a sweetish flavor of pumpkin seeds and tender aroma of summer herbs.

  • Where to buy: LONG JING GREEN TEA
  • Category: Green tea

Tie Guan Yin

Oolong

Oolong combines the freshness and pureness of green tea, the richness and elegance of black tea, and the aromatic aroma of flower tea. It is like all the jewels of the tea world placed in one box. Oolong tea is a mysterious drink, with a unique ability to change its flavor and aroma from one brew to another, each time opening new shades.

Rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, oolong has a positive effect on digestion, lowers the level of cholesterol, and accelerates the metabolism, helping fight excess weight. Tender and aromatic Tie Guan Yin can help take your mind off the stresses of everyday life and sink into a state of peace and ease.

The greenish-yellow brew is filled up with natural nectar sweetness and a long, rich aroma with notes of field flowers.

  • Where to buy: Tie Guan Yin
  • Category: Oolong tea

Black tea

It is hard to surprise us with nice black tea, however, the delicate aroma and rich flavor of Yunnan will satisfy even the most demanding gourmands, opening the magnificence of natural loose-leaf tea.

Lincang Ripe Puerh

Puerh

“LinCang” (“Linj-Tzan”) is the first loose-leaf ripe Pu Er tea in collection of iTea. We have carefully selected it during our trips to Lincang area of Yunnan province. The tea is produced from Yunnan “large leaves” variety, the average age of tea trees is about 40 years.

This tea is of medium fermentation, controlled by the skillful professionals who are in charge of the piling post-fermentation. Since production in 2012, it was properly aged under special conditions.

This puer provides very thick & mellow taste, with long and sweet aftertaste. As it was properly aged in a dry warehouse, the wet and earthy aroma (result of piling during fermentation process) is almost disappeared. Instead, some unique – light and pleasant flavor of dry-stored puer comes out – the hints of nuts, smoked woods and autumn herbs. In the taste of this puer we can also distinguish some dry fruits and spices. Infusion, though rich in color, is clear and transparent.

This puer can be brewed at least 8 times.

  • Where to buy: LINCANG PUERH TEA
  • Category: Puerh

Organic Matcha

Matcha Green Tea

Matcha literally means “powdered tea.” When you brew traditional green tea, components from the leaves get infused into the hot water, then the leaves are discarded. With matcha, you’re drinking the actual leaves, which have been finely powdered and then dissolved in the hot water.

Unlike traditional green tea, production of matcha involves covering the tea plants with shade cloths before they’re harvested. This triggers the growth of leaves with better flavor and texture, which are hand selected, steamed briefly to stop fermentation, then dried and aged in cold storage, which deepens the flavor. The dried leaves are then stone-ground into a fine powder.

The process of brewing of matcha is the focus of Japanese tea ceremonies, and it has long been associated with Zen.

Because matcha is made from high-quality tea, and the whole leaves are ingested, it is even more potent source of useful nutrients than steeped green tea. In addition to providing of vitamins and minerals, matcha is rich in antioxidants called polyphenols, which help to protect against heart diseases and cancer, as well as better blood sugar regulation, blood pressure reduction, and anti-aging. Amongst its many health benefits, matcha boosts metabolism and burns calories, detoxifies effectively and naturally, Provides vitamin C, selenium, chromium, zinc and magnesium. And, most importantly, Matcha calms the mind and relaxes the body, it’s very good tea for meditation.
